# Development approach

*Systems engineering based on stakeholder engagement to elicit user needs*

## Overview

To facilitate input from stakeholders, ISO/TC 204/WG 19 hosted a series of public workshops in late 2021. The results led to the Operational Concept (ConOps), including user needs and constraints. A Committee Draft (CD) was released for committee consultation on 7 June 2022. Based on comments received, terms and definitions were separated into Part 1 (Vocabulary), making ConOps Part 2.

In July 2023, a second ConOps draft was released along with Vocabulary and the System of Systems Requirements and Architecture (SoSR). The SoSR defines requirements for the entire METR network that fulfil ConOps user needs and constraints, and describes the architecture as a system of systems (SoS) with:

1. Regulation system
2. Distribution system
3. Consumer system
4. Discrepancy handling system

Future parts will specify how SoSR requirements are realized by component systems. Another part will define METR data requirements. Interface specifications for interoperability may follow as ISO or regional standards.

## Workshop results

ISO/TC 204/WG 19 held 12 workshops with 47 stakeholders from 13 countries.

![Workshop participants by country](images/pie.png){ width="360" }

We continue to seek input during online ConOps development. Additional meetings have been held since the original workshops, including under-represented categories such as legal stakeholders.
